Who I Am

Engineer turned weaver

I left Google to build something harder — community infrastructure for a world that needs it. That path led through a Boulder City Council race, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, a cooperative co-working space, and now a graduate program in Creative Technology & Design at CU Boulder's ATLAS Institute.

My work lives at a strange and fertile edge: where mycorrhizal thinking meets distributed systems, where taiji philosophy informs product design, where contemplative practice shapes how we build AI.

I'm building Parachute.Computer — a voice-first personal knowledge management tool with a living knowledge graph I call Parachute Brain. It's an extension of self in the sense that Andy Clark and David Chalmers meant: the mind reaching beyond the skull.

My personal AI agent is named UnforcedAGI — Aaron Gabriel's Intelligence — a reflection of my belief that the most powerful AI is deeply personal, contextual, and grown alongside us rather than imposed upon us.

Parachute.Computer

Voice-First Knowledge Management · Open Parachute PBC

A voice-first personal knowledge management tool built around Parachute Brain — a living knowledge graph that grows with you. Built on NVIDIA's Parakeet ASR, Nemotron reasoning models, and a conversational ontology discovery approach. The vision: shared ownership of digital knowledge infrastructure as a public benefit.
